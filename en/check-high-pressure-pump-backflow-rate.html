Check High-Pressure Pump Backflow Rate

Document ID: 5219 | Version: 2.5 | Last Modified Date: Sep 10, 2025

Required Tools:

  • Fuel Circuit Pressure Check Kit (212542)

  • Cursor Engine Fuel Leakage Test Kit Add-On (Part Number 73470B)

  • Stopwatch

Procedure:

  1. Prepare to check the high-pressure pump backflow rate.

    High-Pressure Pump Connectors

    A

    Low-Pressure Gear Pump

    B

    High-Pressure Pump

    C

    Return to Main Filter (Total Backflow)

    D

    Inlet Supply from Main Filter

    E

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP)

    F

    Inlet from ECU Cooling Plate

    G

    Outlet to Main Filter

    H

    Outlet to Fuel Rail

    1. Visually check all related components for any signs of leakage.

    2. Disconnect the tube at the total backflow port of the high-pressure pump

    3. Install a blanking plug on the end tube connected to the high-pressure pump total backflow port.

    4. Connect a clear flexible tube to the high-pressure pump total backflow port.

    5. Prepare the measuring cup and stopwatch.

  2. Check high-pressure pump backflow rate.

    1. Crank/start the engine until the diesel runs out of the tube and into the measuring cup.

    2. Start the engine and start the stopwatch.

    3. In case of non-starter: Crank engine multiple times (maximum 10 seconds each) until a specific time period is reached.

      Notice

      To prevent damaging the starter, do not engage the starter motor for more than 10–12 seconds each time. Wait 30 seconds between each attempt to START the engine.

    4. Fill the measuring cup while running the engine for the duration and conditions listed in the table below. Compare the total volume of diesel in the measuring cup to the expected results.

      Engine Condition

      Volume

      Low Speed (750 rpm) for 60 s

      600–800 ml (20–27 oz)

  3. Check if the volumes measured are in the range explained in the table above.

    1. If the expected results are NOT OK (volume rate is noticeably too low), investigate these possible root causes:

      • Insufficient fuel

      • Check the tightness of tubes and connectors. Even a small defect in tightness will lead to a reduced suction pressure. Air will be sucked into the system and the diesel flow will be reduced. (Check that there are no air bubbles in the backflow from the pump.)

      • Check tubes, connectors squeezed/damaged/wrong diameter. (If there are no bubbles in the backflow, check the pipeline).

      • The main filter is blocked. (If delta pressure is over 40 kPa (6 psi), replace the main filter).

      • Tank ventilation is blocked or restricted. Open the tank cap, and listen for a sucking sound.

      Locate and correct the cause of the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the expected results are OK, continue to the next step.

  4. The high-pressure pump works properly.
