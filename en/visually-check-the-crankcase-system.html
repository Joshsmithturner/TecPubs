Visually Check the Crankcase System

Document ID: 3970 | Version: 2.3 | Last Modified Date: Oct 3, 2025

Preparation:

  • Ignition key is in the STOP position.

Procedure:

  1. Visually check the oil filler cap for pollution and blow-by.

    • Check if there is vapour coming out.

    • Visually check for emulsion (condensation and fuel).

    1. If there is pollution or blow-by, investigate these possible root causes:

      • If emulsion is visible, the oil mist is clogged or incorrect oil type or viscosity

      Locate and correct the cause of the pollution or blow-by, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If there is no pollution or blow-by, continue to the next step.

  2. Visually check the ventilation lines for damage, leaks, pollution, and blockage.

    Blockages in the crankcase system may lead to higher boost pressure.

    • Check if the oil separator is clogged.

    • Check the lines for damage, cracks, age, or blockage.

    • Check the rubber seals for damage or age.

    1. If the crankcase system is NOT OK, locate and correct the damage, leaks, and blockage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the crankcase system is OK, continue to the next step.

  3. Replace the crankcase pressure sensor.

    Replace the crankcase pressure sensor, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.

    1. If the fault has been resolved, continue to the next step.

    2. If the fault is not resolved, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help.

  4. The crankcase system works properly.
