SPN-27426-31 / DTC-6B22-1F

Starter Control Relay: Short Circuit to Battery on Low Side

Document ID: 6939 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Oct 29, 2024

Fault Description: 

The electronic control unit (ECU) controls the voltage towards the starter control relay. The starter control relay controls the voltage of the starter relay. The ECU monitors the starter control relay driver circuit to check for a short circuit to battery condition. If the ECU detects a short circuit to battery in the low side driver circuit, this fault occurs.

Cause: 

The ECU has detected a short circuit to battery condition in the starter control relay circuit, low side driver.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Faulty starter control relay, internal failure.

  2. Faulty starter control relay driver circuit, short circuit to battery condition.

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fault is present and active using either the PT-Box tool or LogOn, then retrieve the IQAN clone.

    1. If the fault is present and active, continue to the next step.

    2. If the fault is no longer present or in an inactive state, the fault may be intermittent. Continue to the next step.

  2. Check the starter control relay coil for an internal failure.

    Remove the starter control relay.

    Use a multimeter to measure the resistance on the starter control relay coil.

    From

    To

    Value

    Starter Control Relay position 85

    Starter Control Relay position 86

    70–130 Ω

    1. If the value is infinite, the starter control relay has failed internally. Replace the starter control relay.

    2. If the value is in the specified range, leave the relay disconnected and continue to the next step.

  3. Check the starter control relay low side driver circuit for a short circuit to battery condition.

    Disconnect the ECU connector-1 (machine harness connector).

    With the key in the RUN position, use a multimeter to perform the following voltage check:

    From

    To

    Value

    ECU Connector-1 (Plug) position 88

    Chassis ground

    There should be no voltage.

    ECU Connector-1 (Plug) position 88

    All other positions in ECU Connector-1 (Machine Harness Connector)

    There should be no voltage.

    1. If there is voltage, there is a short circuit to battery condition in the starter control relay low side driver circuit. Locate and repair the shorted conductor.

    2. If there is no voltage, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help.

  4. Visually inspect the relevant harnesses and connectors for bent or dislocated pins, corroded terminals, broken wires, and signs of heat damage or pinch damage to the harness braiding. Verify that the connectors are fully installed. Flex the harnesses involved to reveal intermittent breaks or shorts in the wiring. Operate the machine while you monitor the display.

    1. If you find damage or the display indicates abnormal display readings, repair the damage discovered during the inspection or locate and repair the abnormal display condition, then verify that the fault has been resolved.

    2. If you do not find damage and the display indicates only normal readings, delete the fault code and return the machine to service.
