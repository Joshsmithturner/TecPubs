Check Intake Air Pressure and Temperature Sensor

Document ID: 5124 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Oct 3, 2025

Preparation:

  • Ignition key is in the STOP position.

Procedure:

  1. Visually check the intake air pressure and temperature sensor installation.

    • Visually check the sensor connector for damage or contamination.

    • Check that the sensor is mounted in the correct orientation.

    • Check if the screw of the sensor is tight.

    • Check if the sensor is correctly fitted to the intake air manifold.

    1. If the intake air pressure and temperature sensor is NOT OK, locate and correct the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the intake air pressure and temperature sensor is OK, continue to the next step.

  2. Read out the intake air pressure value.

    With the key in the RUN position and the engine off, use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to read out the boost pressure value. The value should equal atmospheric pressure (approximately 1.01 bar or 14.7 psi at sea level).
