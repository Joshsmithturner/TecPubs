Check Fuel Rail Pressure Adjustment

Document ID: 5220 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Oct 23, 2024

Preparation:

  • Check that the battery voltage is sufficient.

  • Check that the vehicle fuses are okay.

  • Check that the connectors and plugs are okay. There should be no loose contact or corrosion.

Procedure:

  1. Check the fuel rail pressure adjustment.

    1. Turn the key to the RUN position.

    2. Connect the PT-box tool.

    3. Using the PT-Box tool, start Active Diagnosis > Rail Pressure to check and modify the fuel rail pressure.

      Fuel rail pressure adjustment should be possible in the range of:

      • 250–1600 bar (3625–23206 psi)

    4. If the fuel rail pressure adjustment is not OK, investigate these possible root causes:

      • Electrical defect of fuel system's components (such as fuel rail pressure sensor, fuel pump, fuel injector). Check the wiring harness and component electrically.

      • Mechanical defect of fuel system's components (such as faulty fuel pressure limiting valve).

      • Clogged fuel filters.

      Locate and correct the cause, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    5. If the fuel rail pressure adjustment is OK, continue to the next step.

  2. The injector works properly.
