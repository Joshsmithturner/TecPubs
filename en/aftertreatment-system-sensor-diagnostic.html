Aftertreatment System Sensor Diagnostic

(7 pin Inline Connector)

Document ID: 5465 | Version: 3.7 | Last Modified Date: May 14, 2025

Note

Before starting work, read and follow all servicing safety precautions as listed in the machine and attachment operator and service manuals.

Note

Diagnostic results are as accurate as the quality of the measuring tools. Maintain the integrity and cleanliness of the 7-pin inline test harness to ensure the accuracy of the values recorded during diagnostics.

Use a multimeter of good quality when taking electrical measurements. Even a good ohm meter must be zeroed before testing. This means that the resistance with just the leads touching together must be subtracted from each measurement.

Test harness kit 81768B assists with CAN diagnostics for aftertreatment system (ATS) components at the inline (7 pin) connector located beside the engine ECU. This will help prevent inadvertent damage to the connector and its pins caused with incorrect multi-meter probe sizes and back probing of the connector during testing. The test harness has multimeter test terminals for ATS Power (Red), ATS Ground (Black), CAN Hi (Yellow), and CAN Lo (Green).

A pin drag test can be performed with the pin drag test kit (part number 81758B) included in the harness kit 81768B.

The following steps outline a systematic approach to test the ATS circuit for CAN Bus engine codes. Record your results at the end of each step and have them ready if contacting the Tigercat Industries Service Department.

Part 1: Terminal Tension and Initial CAN Bus Circuit Test

Note

For intermittent faults, the harness should be wriggled while monitoring the meter during all of the following steps. This may require more than one person to perform correctly.

  1. Turn the ignition key to the STOP position and wait for 90 seconds.

  2. Check the 7 pin inline connector for damage or loose connections.

    Disconnect the 7-pin inline connector and visually inspect for damage. Check the tension of the receptacle terminals using the appropriate terminals from the pin drag test kit. If the terminals do not hold proper tension on the pins, they can create a loose connection, resulting in a fault.

    1. If a loose connection is found, replace the loose terminals and check if the fault is still active.

    2. If no damage or loose connections are found, continue to the next step.

  3. Install the test harness between the 7 pin inline connector and the machine harness connector.

    With the key in the RUN position, use a multimeter to measure the voltage across the red and black test ports of the test harness.
