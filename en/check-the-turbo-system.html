Check the Turbo System

Document ID: 4185 | Version: 2.3 | Last Modified Date: Mar 25, 2025

Required Tools:

  • Pressure hand pump

Procedure:

  1. Check the turbo charger.

    Notice

    Take special care to not damage the turbo. Use clean gloves when touching the turbo wheel blade. Only use a small amount of force to move the wheel.

    Air Turbo Charger Location

    • START the engine in low speed and check the turbo charger for mechanical sounds, then STOP the engine.

    • Dismount the air intake boot, charge air cooler pipe, and exhaust tube from the turbo to access the turbo charger compressor and the turbine wheel blades.

    • Visually check the turbo charger for debris.

    • Check manually the rotation of the turbo charger wheel by moving it gently.

    • Check manually the bearing of the turbo charger by feeling if it moves under pressure.

    1. If the turbo is damaged, investigate these possible root causes:

      • Bearing of the turbo charger is damaged.

      • Compressor or turbine wheels of the turbo are blocked or stiff.

      • Turbo charger is damaged.

      Locate and correct the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the turbo charger is not damaged, continue to the next step.

  2. Check the wastegate valve.

    Wastegate Valve

    1

    Wastegate Valve Hose

    2

    Wastegate Valve

    • Visually check the wastegate valve for debris.

    • Check if the hose of the wastegate valve is loose or damaged.

    • Use a pressure hand pump to check the correct actuation of the wastegate valve.

      Pump and monitor gradually the pressure at wastegate valve in order to check if the actuation of the valve is reached at a pressure less than 2.5 bar (absolute pressure). The air pressure changes constantly depending on altitude or weather conditions. Therefore, the pressure is measured absolutely. The pressure is always related to absolute zero - the absolute vacuum [p(abs)=p(amb)+p(bar)].

    1. If the wastegate valve is damaged, investigate these possible root causes:

      • Wastegate valve hose is loose or damaged.

      • Wastegate valve leverage is blocked or damaged.

      • Wastegate valve is blocked or damaged (probably caused by damaged valve spring).

      Locate and correct the cause of the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the wastegate valve is not damaged, continue to the next step.

  3. The turbo charger works properly.
