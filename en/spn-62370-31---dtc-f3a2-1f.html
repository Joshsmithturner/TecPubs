SPN-62370-31 / DTC-F3A2-1F

Info: Torque Limitation Caused by Excessive Coolant Temperature

Document ID: 7678 | Version: 2.1 | Last Modified Date: May 14, 2025

Fault Description: 

This fault is generated for information purposes. A limitation of performance has been activated due to a high coolant temperature lasting more than 120 seconds, which was not caused by a failure, but by extreme operating conditions.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Radiator clogged.

  2. Coolant leak.

  3. Problem in the coolant flow (water pump or tubes).

  4. Faulty fan transmission.

  5. Low coolant level.

  6. Air in the coolant.

  7. Thermostat blocked in the closed position.

  8. Leak from the head gasket.

  9. External environmental conditions.

Solution:

  • This fault does not require any action except to check if other faults concerning the cooling system are present in the fault memory. If the power reduction has been caused by an actual fault, the fault that has activated the torque limitation must be present in the fault memory. Follow the troubleshooting procedure for the actual fault. No actions are necessary due to this fault alone.
