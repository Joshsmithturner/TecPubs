Check the Engine Speed Adjustment

Document ID: 5244 | Version: 3.1 | Last Modified Date: Oct 23, 2024

Procedure:

This procedure is used to modify and maintain the engine speed. Adjustment is possible in the range from idle to 2300 rpm.

  1. Prepare to check the engine speed adjustment.

    • Check that the battery voltage is sufficient.

    • Check that the vehicle fuses are OK.

    • Check that the connectors and plugs are okay. There should be no loose contact or corrosion.

    • Start the engine by turning the key to the START position.

    • Connect the PT-Box tool and navigate to Active Diagnosis, then Engine Speed.

  2. Check the engine speed adjustment.

    Use the PT-Box tool to modify and maintain the engine speed from idle to 2300 rpm.

    Check that the actual engine speed matches the speed set on the screen.

    1. If the engine speed adjustment check is NOT OK, investigate these possible root causes:

      • Engine air system fault (such as air leak or faulty turbocharger).

      • Engine fuel system fault (such as leaking injector or faulty fuel pump).

      • Engine/transmission fault (such as head cylinder pressure leaking or wrong cam crank timing).

      Locate and correct the cause of the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the engine speed adjustment check is OK, continue to the next step.

  3. The engine speed adjustment check works properly.
