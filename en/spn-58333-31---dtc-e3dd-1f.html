SPN-58333-31 / DTC-E3DD-1F

ECU Internal: Diagnosis Fault Check to Report the Error to Demand for an ICO Due to an Error in the PoI3 Efficiency Factor

Document ID: 6067 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Apr 15, 2025

Fault Description: 

The engine performance depends on different injections during the same combustion cycle: pilot injections, main injections, and three post-injections. The electronic control unit (ECU) monitors post-injection 3 efficiency.

Cause: 

The ECU has detected an unwanted increase in the engine speed.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Faulty fuel injectors, leaking.

  2. Faulty piston rings, engine oil leaking into cylinders.

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fault is present and active using either the PT-Box tool or LogOn, then retrieve the IQAN clone.

  2. Check for leaking fuel injectors. With the fuel pressure leak kit, install the injector backflow line and route to an appropriate container, then bring the engine up to operating temperature. At low idle, injector backflow should be 80 ml per minute maximum. Refer to Fuel Injector Leakage Test for the full procedure.

    1. If injector backflow is greater than 80 ml per minute, use the fuel rail injector plug kit to isolate each injector, one at a time. Determine which injectors are faulty and replace them.

    2. If injector backflow is less than or equal to 80 ml per minute, continue to the next step.

  3. Perform a cylinder compression test. Refer to Cylinder Compression Test for the full procedure.

    1. If failures are detected during the compression test, repair or replace components according to the troubleshooting procedure.

    2. If no failures are detected in the compression test, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help.
