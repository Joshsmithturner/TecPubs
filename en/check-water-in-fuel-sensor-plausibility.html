Check Water In Fuel Sensor Plausibility

Document ID: 5181 | Version: 2.3 | Last Modified Date: Oct 28, 2024

Procedure:

  1. Check the fuel/water separator for visible damage.

  2. Check water in fuel sensor plausibility.

    Fuel/Water Separator

    A

    Drain Valve with Hose Barb

    B

    Water in Fuel Sensor

    C

    Fuel Heater

    1. Use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to read the fault code memory from the ECU and determine if SPN-17429-31 / DTC-4415-1F is active.

    2. Check the amount of water at the water in fuel sensor.

    3. Check the table below to determine if the water in fuel sensor is OK.

      Fault Status (SPN-17429 / DTC-4415)

      Water Visible in Sensor

      Sensor Status

      Active

      Yes

      OK

      No

      NOT OK

      Not Active

      Yes

      NOT OK

      No

      OK

    4. If the water in fuel sensor is NOT OK, investigate these possible root causes:

      • Electrical defect of the water in fuel sensor, replace the component.

      Locate and correct the cause, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    5. If the water in fuel sensor is OK, continue to the next step.

  3. The water in fuel sensor works properly.
