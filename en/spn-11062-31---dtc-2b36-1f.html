SPN-11062-31 / DTC-2B36-1F

Fuel Injector: Minimum Fuel Rail Pressure has Not Been Reached

Fault Description: 

The electronic control unit (ECU) monitors the fuel rail pressure sensor to check for the presence of the minimum pressure required to allow fuel injection. If the ECU determines that fuel pressure is below the minimum level, fuel injection is not permitted and this fault occurs.

Cause: 

The minimum fuel rail pressure value necessary to allow fuel injection has not been reached.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Faulty fuel rail pressure sensor, wiring or internal failure.

  2. Faulty engine injection system, low pressure side (clogged fuel filter or leakage/blockage).

  3. Faulty gear pump, pressure too low.

  4. Faulty fuel metering valve (MPROP), wiring or internal failure.

  5. Faulty high-pressure pump.

  6. Faulty pressure relief valve (PRV), stuck open.

  7. Faulty 5 bar pressure relief valve.

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fuel injectors are operating correctly by performing the following procedures:

    • Check High-Pressure Pump Backflow Rate

    • Check Leakage of High-Pressure System

    • Fuel Injector Leakage Test

    • Fuel Pressure Check

    • Fuel High Pressure Relief Test

  2. If the cause of the fault has not been identified, perform the following procedures:

    • Check High-Pressure Pump Backflow Pressure

    • Check Suction Pressure After Pre-Filter

    • Check Suction Pressure Between Tank and Pre-Filter

    • Check For External Fuel Leakage in the Engine Area
