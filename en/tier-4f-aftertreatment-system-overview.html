Tier 4f Aftertreatment System Overview

Document ID: 28215 | Version: 2.1 | Last Modified Date: Jun 11, 2025

This machine is equipped with a combination Diesel Oxidation Catalyst (DOC) and high-efficiency Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) aftertreatment system. The aftertreatment system limits the nitrogen oxide exhaust gas values (NOx) to meet Tier 4f emission standards with low fuel consumption.

The DOC/SCR aftertreatment system is electronically managed by the engine electronic control unit (ECU). The ECU calculates and adjusts the flow rate of the DEF solution into the system based on current engine speed, torque delivered, exhaust temperature, and amount of NOx and ammonia (NH3) present.

The aftertreatment system is essentially composed of the following:

  • The Diesel Oxidation Catalyst (DOC), which uses oxidation at high temperatures to convert the exhaust gas in the first stage of the process. The DOC also incorporates a mixing tube where DEF is mixed with the exhaust gases.

  • The Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) catalytic converter, which converts the exhaust gas in the second stage of the process. The SCR also incorporates a clean-up catalyst which reduces the amount of excess ammonia (NH3) resulting from the process.

  • Three Exhaust Gas Temperature Sensors are located at the DOC inlet, the SCR inlet, and the SCR outlet.

  • Two NOx Sensors are located at the DOC inlet and the SCR outlet.

  • NH3 Sensor which detects NH3 (ammonia) is located at the exhaust gas outlet.

  • The DEF Tank holds the reactive solution, diesel exhaust fluid (DEF).

  • The Coolant Control Valve diverts coolant to heat the DEF system.

  • The DEF Supply Module pumps DEF from the DEF tank to the DEF dosing module.

  • The DEF Dosing Module injects DEF into the DOC.

  • The Air Humidity and Temperature Sensor (not shown) is located on the engine intake pipe downstream of the air cleaner.

Exhaust Gas Aftertreatment Process

  1. The exhaust passes through the DOC. The DOC uses oxidation at high temperatures to convert carbon monoxide (CO), hydrocarbons (HC) and the soluble organic fraction (SOF) of diesel particulates into inert compounds, carbon dioxide (CO2) and water vapour (H2O). It also converts nitrogen oxide (NO) into nitrogen dioxide (NO2). Increased NO2 levels enhance the performance of the SCR catalyst at low temperatures and increase the effectiveness of the DOC/SCR aftertreatment system as a whole.

  2. The supply module pump picks up the diesel exhaust fluid (DEF) solution from the tank and sends it under pressure through the dosing module filter to the dosing module (DEF injector) to be injected into the exhaust at the outlet of the DOC and pass through the mixer tube upstream of the catalytic converter.

  3. The first phase of the SCR process takes place in the pipe between the DOC and the catalytic converter. High temperature exhaust gas evaporates the DEF solution instantly and, by hydrolysis, converts it into NH3 and CO2. Evaporation of the DEF solution lowers the exhaust gas temperature, bringing it closer to the optimum temperature required by the process.

  4. The exhaust gases and NH3, at the proper reaction temperature, are introduced into the catalytic converter, where the second phase of the process takes place. The NH3 is converted into free nitrogen (N2) and H2O by reacting with the oxygen in the exhaust gases.

  5. Finally, the high-efficiency catalytic converter incorporates a clean up catalyst (CUC) which reduces any excess ammonia (NH3) resulting from the SCR process.
