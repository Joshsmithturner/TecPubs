SPN-60382-31 / DTC-EBDE-1F

NH3 Sensor: Electrical Fault on Trim Resistor Line

Document ID: 10649 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Oct 29, 2024

Note

Because this fault causes inducement, it is necessary to perform the Inducement Unlock/Counter Reset once the fault has been resolved. Contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department if needed.

Fault Description: 

The NH3 ECU is required to send information at regular intervals to the electronic control unit (ECU) via Controller Area Network (CAN). If the reference compensation resistor circuit is compromised in any way, this fault occurs.

Cause: 

The NH3 ECU has reported, via CAN, to the ECU that an open circuit, short circuit to ground, or short circuit to voltage source condition exists in the reference trim resistor control circuit.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Faulty NH3 sensor, internal failure.

  2. Faulty NH3 ECU, hardware or firmware.

Suggested Spare Parts:

  • NH3 sensor

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fault is present and active using either the PT-Box tool or LogOn, then retrieve the IQAN clone.

    1. If the fault is present and active, continue to the next step.

    2. If the fault is no longer present or in an inactive state, the fault may be intermittent. Continue to Step 3.

  2. Check the condition of the trim resistor in the NH3 ECU to NH3 sensor harness (cable).

    Disconnect the NH3 ECU to NH3 sensor harness (cable) at the NH3 sensor connector.

    Use a multimeter to measure resistance on the NH3 sensor harness (cable) side.

    1. If the measured resistance is not within the specified range or there is continuity to any other position, the NH3 sensor trim resistor has failed or the wiring harness is damaged. Replace the NH3 sensor and its connecting cable.

    2. If the measured resistance is within range and there is no continuity to any of the other positions, continue to the next step.

  3. Visually inspect the relevant harnesses and connectors for bent or dislocated pins, corroded terminals, broken wires, and signs of heat damage or pinch damage to the harness braiding. Verify that the connectors are fully installed. Flex the harnesses involved to reveal intermittent breaks or shorts in the wiring. Operate the machine while you monitor the display.

    1. If you find damage or the display indicates abnormal display readings, repair the damage discovered during the inspection or locate and repair the abnormal display condition, then verify that the fault has been resolved.

    2. If you do not find damage and the display indicates abnormal display readings, continue to the next step.

    3. If you do not find damage and the display indicates only normal readings, delete the fault code and return the machine to service.

  4. Replace the NH3 sensor, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.

    1. If the fault has been resolved, return the machine to service.

    2. If the fault is not resolved, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help.
