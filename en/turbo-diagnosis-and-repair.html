Turbo Diagnosis and Repair

Document ID: 2415 | Version: 3.10 | Last Modified Date: Oct 23, 2024

Note

Before starting work, read and follow all servicing safety precautions as listed in the machine and attachment operator and service manuals.

Description: Diagnosing and repair guidelines to assist technicians with turbo concerns and failures.

Required Action: Review and follow the steps below during turbo diagnosis and repair.

Notice

When removing a silicone or rubber elbow or intake connector, carefully inspect it for any delamination between the layers before reinstalling it on the machine. If there is any sign of delamination or damage, replace the part before putting the machine back in service.

New Silicone Elbow

Damaged Silicone Elbow

When installing the elbow or intake connector, make sure it is seated correctly on the pipe or turbo before tightening the clamps. The clamp should be located right behind the bead, not on top of the bead or too close to the edge of the connector.

Note

The following information must be sent to Tigercat during any turbo diagnosis or repair for warranty consideration. Please review and ensure all requested documents are readable before sending them to Tigercat.

  • Serial Number

  • Machine hours

  • Data stored/trip recorder (taken with PT-Box)

  • Fault codes (taken with PT-Box)

  • IQAN clone with logs

  • Oil sample before draining

  • Copy of all tests performed

  • *CCV filters must be submitted with the turbo

Possible Failure Modes:

Review the following failure modes to the existing complaint and then proceed to the corresponding number below to follow the required diagnosis and repair steps.

  1. Catastrophic Failure

  2. Lack of Performance

  3. Black/White Smoke in the Exhaust

  4. Oil Leaking From Compressor Inlet

  5. Oil Leaking From Compressor Outlet

  6. Oil Leaking From Turbine Outlet

  7. Excessive Oil in the Exhaust System (Turbo Replacement)

  8. Engine Oil Consumption
