SPN-62021-31 / DTC-F245-1F

Fuel Pressure Relief Valve: Pressure Increase Forcing Relief Valve Open

Document ID: 10745 | Version: 2.4 | Last Modified Date: Sep 10, 2025

Fault Description: 

To determine the mechanical wear or to switch the system to emergency mode, the electronic control unit (ECU) can force the fuel pressure relief valve (PRV) open. This action involves two steps: waiting for the fuel rail pressure to increase, followed by a sudden change in pressure (pressure shock). If the ECU commands this operation, this fault occurs. Once a fault has been detected by the fuel rail pressure sensor or the maximum fuel rail pressure has been exceeded, the system goes into emergency mode. If this fault occurs, other fuel system related faults may occur due to this increase in fuel pressure.

Solution:

  1. Check the status of the following related faults:

    SPN-5157-31 / DTC-1425-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Sensor: Voltage is Too High

    SPN-7557-31 / DTC-1D85-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure has Exceeded the Maximum Limit During Limp Home Mode

    SPN-8805-31 / DTC-2265-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Has Exceeded Maximum Positive Deviation Limits

    SPN-15205-31 / DTC-3B65-1F

    High-Pressure Pump Fuel Delivery Quantity in Over Run Exceeds a Maximum Threshold

    SPN-15461-31 / DTC-3C65-1F

    High Pressure Pump Fuel Delivery Quantity at Low Speed is Too High

    SPN-21077-31 / DTC-5255-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Open Circuit

    SPN-33621-31 / DTC-8355-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Over-Temperature Error

    SPN-62277-31 / DTC-F345-1F

    Fuel Pressure Relief Valve: Pressure Spike Forcing Relief Valve Open

    SPN-62533-31 / DTC-F445-1F

    Fuel Pressure Relief Valve is Open

    1. If any of the listed faults are active, resolve them first and then determine if this fault is also resolved.

    2. If none of the listed faults are active, continue to the next step.

  2. Check the correct operation of the fuel injectors.

    Perform the following procedures:

    • Check High-Pressure Pump Backflow Pressure

    • Fuel Injector Leakage Test

    • Fuel High Pressure Relief Test

    1. If any problems are detected during the test procedures, resolve them first and then determine if this fault is resolved.

    2. If no problems are found, continue to the next step.

  3. Check the fuel rail pressure sensor and replace the sensor if any malfunctions are detected.
