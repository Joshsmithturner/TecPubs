Engine Oil Consumption

Document ID: 27371 | Version: 2.12 | Last Modified Date: Oct 7, 2025

Note

Allow a minimum of 500 engine hours break-in period before performing this procedure.

Note

DO NOT replace the engine unless authorized by the Tigercat Industries Service Department.

This troubleshooting procedure is completed in four phases:

  1. Perform the Dealer Procedure (0 Hours).

  2. Operate the machine normally for 250 hours and record every time engine oil is added to the machine. See Operator Procedure (Oil Consumption Recording After Replacing the Oil).

  3. Perform the Dealer Procedure (250 Hours) and send all recorded data to the Tigercat Industries Service Department.

  4. If oil consumption is deemed excessive by Tigercat Industries, perform the Crankcase Pressure Test.

Dealer Procedure (0 Hours)

  1. Perform a full visual inspection of the engine to identify and repair any leaks before starting. Pay particular attention to the crank seals and the turbo inlet/outlet ports shown below.     • If not equipped with an air filter restriction sensor, read the air intake vacuum reading (kPa/inH2O) directly from the air filter restriction indicator.

  7. Replace the crankcase ventilation (CCV) filter if the previous interval is greater than 500 hours.

  8. Begin data recording at the next oil service interval after taking an oil sample, replacing the oil, and replacing the filter.

  9. Position the machine on flat, level ground.

  10. Engine oil level dipstick validation.

    1. Drain oil, replace the filter.

    2. Measure out the required amount of 15W40 engine oil and then fill the sump through the oil filler neck (not rocker cover). DO NOT assume that the oil supplier container has the stated volume.

      Engine

      Oil Sump at 'MAX' Line On the Dipstick (Litres)

      N45

      10.5

      N67

      14

      C87

      25

    3. Confirm oil is at the 'MAX' line on the dipstick and contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department if it is not.

    4. Run the engine for several minutes.

    5. Add the necessary oil to raise the level to the 'MAX' line on the dipstick.

  11. Retrieve the following data:

    • Machine serial number

    • Machine hours

    • Engine hours

    • Clone with logs (IQAN)

    • Data stored (PT-Box)

    • Fault codes (PT-Box)

    • The vacuum reading from the filter restriction indicator before and after cleaning/checking the pre-cleaner and main filter.

  12. Wait until the machine has operated for 250 hours, then perform the Dealer Procedure (250 Hours).

Operator Procedure (Oil Consumption Recording After Replacing the Oil)

  1. Record information in the Oil Consumption Chart at the end of this procedure; all fields are mandatory.

  2. Park the machine on level ground and check the oil at the start of every shift BEFORE starting the engine.

  3. If oil is added, record volume, date, and engine hours.

  4. After 250 hours, notify the dealer to perform a final inspection.

Dealer Procedure (250 Hours)

  1. After 250 hours, record the air intake vacuum reading in the oil consumption chart.

  2. Retrieve and submit a second oil sample to the same company as the previous oil sample.

  3. Retrieve the following data:

    • Machine serial number

    • Machine hours

    • Engine hours

    • Clone with logs (IQAN)

    • Data stored (PT-Box)

    • Fault codes (PT-Box)

    • The vacuum reading from the filter restriction indicator.

  4. Send all data recorded during the Dealer Procedure (0 Hours) and during the previous step (250 hours) to the Tigercat Industries Service Department. If oil consumption is deemed excessive by Tigercat Industries, perform the Crankcase Pressure Test.

Crankcase Pressure Test

The following procedure outlines steps to validate engine crankcase pressure on Tigercat by FPT engines while using the manometer tool kit 88785B. Refer to the instruction manual provided with the kit for further details.

Required Tools

  • Manometer tool kit (88785B)

  1. Replace the CCV filter(s).

  2. Install a short hose from the kit with plug to block the CCV port at the air intake tube.

  3. Keep one end of the CCV hose connected to the CCV filter box and the other connected to the T-fitting from the Manometer kit. Connect the Manometer hose to the T-fitting. Install the Manometer on the frame of the machine.

  4. Loosen both shut-off valves counterclockwise 1/2 turn before connecting a 1/4 in tube to any of the two fittings.

  5. Take the crankcase pressure measurements:

    A

    Fluid Equalized (Engine Stopped)

    B

    Crankcase Pressure with Engine at Low Speed

    C

    Crankcase Pressure with Engine at High Speed

    Note

    The images above are for reference only and may not reflect what you see on your machine. These images show crankcase pressure measurement at 0.14 kPa (0.6 inH2O) (the difference between the high and low sides of the manometer). Excessive crankcase pressure was measured at 2.5 kPa (10 inH2O).

    1. With the engine STOPPED, confirm that the fluid is equalized on each side of the scale; re-position the manometer if needed.

    2. Measure and record crankcase pressure with the machine at operating temperature at low speed under no load. Allow the reading to stabilize for 1 minute, then record the difference between the two sides of the manometer.

    3. Measure and record crankcase pressure with the machine at operating temperature at high speed under no load. Allow the reading to stabilize for 1 minute, then record the difference between the two sides of the manometer.

  6. Disconnect the 1/4 in tube and tighten both shut-off valves shut by turning clockwise.

  7. Remove the short hose from the air intake pipe and reconnect the CCV hose from the filter box.

Dealer Checklist (Complete All Actions to Ensure Accuracy of Test Results)
START OF 250 HOURS INTERVAL

  • Visual inspection; take a picture and submit it to Tigercat Industries.

  • Record intake vacuum reading in the Oil Consumption Chart (Start of 250 Hours reading).

  • Check/replace CCV filter; note in the oil consumption chart.

  • Replace oil and filter (15W40 oil); note in the oil consumption chart.

  • Validate dipstick level.

  • Take an oil sample of new oil and submit it to an oil sampling company.

  • Record and save data:

    • Machine Serial Number

    • Machine Hours

    • Engine Hours

    • Clone with Logs

    • Data Store

    • Fault codes

END OF 250 HOURS INTERVAL

  • Record intake vacuum reading in the chart Oil Consumption Chart (End of 250 Hours reading).

  • Take an oil sample of used oil and submit it to the same sampling company as the first sample.

  • Record and save data a second time:

    • Machine Serial Number

    • Machine Hours

    • Engine Hours

    • Clone with Logs

    • Data Store

    • Fault codes

  • Record crankcase pressure (if required by the Tigercat Industries Service Department)
