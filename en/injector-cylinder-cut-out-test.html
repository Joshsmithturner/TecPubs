Injector Cylinder Cut Out Test

Document ID: 5164 | Version: 2.1 | Last Modified Date: Sep 12, 2024

Preparation:

  • Check that the battery voltage is sufficient.

  • Check that the vehicle fuses are okay.

  • Check that the connectors and plugs are okay. There should be no loose contact or corrosion.

  • START the engine and connect the PT-Box tool.

Procedure:

  1. Check the injector cut out.

    Using the PT-Box tool, deactivate a single fuel injector so the injector no longer delivers fuel to the cylinder.

    Observe the change in system behaviour, such as changes in sound, increasing vibration, change in fuel consumption, or change in engine speed.

    • If an injector which is not delivering sufficient quantity is deactivated, the system behaviour should not change greatly.

    • If a working injector is deactivated, the sound will change and the engine will shake noticeably.

  2. Repeat the injector cut out for each cylinder.

    1. If the injector cut out activation is NOT OK, investigate these possible root causes:

      • Electrical defect of the injector, check wiring harness and component electrically.

      • Mechanical defect of the injector, such as seizing.

      • Mechanical defect of the engine, cylinder head gasket damaged, or valve control damaged.

      Locate and repair the cause, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the injector cut out activation is OK, continue to the next step.

  3. The injector works properly.
