Fuel Pressure Relief Valve is Open

Fault Description: 

A mechanical pressure relief valve (PRV), integrated in the fuel rail, opens if excessive pressure is present in the fuel rail. The electronic control unit (ECU) detects and counts the sudden pressure drops in the fuel rail due to an opening of the PRV. If a valve opening is detected, this fault occurs.

Low inlet pressure or inlet restriction can also induce this code. The low-pressure checks should be performed first.

Cause: 

The ECU has detected that the PRV is open.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Low inlet pressure or inlet restriction.

  2. The ECU has commanded the PRV open by a pressure shock.

  3. Faulty fuel injectors.

  4. Faulty high-pressure pump.

  5. Faulty fuel metering valve (MPROP), wiring or internal failure.

  6. Faulty PRV.

  7. Faulty fuel rail pressure sensor, implausible signal.

  8. Faulty low-pressure pump line, damaged or clogged.

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fault is present and active using either the PT-Box tool or LogOn, then retrieve the IQAN clone.

  2. Check for low inlet pressure and inlet restriction.

    1. Replace the fuel filter and the fuel filter/water separator filter element.

    2. Remove the inlet line from between the high-pressure pump and the fuel filter.

    3. Install a fuel pressure test gauge at the filter outlet and the high-pressure pump inlet.

    4. Check the fuel pressure reading at idle and high idle. The pressure should be at least 5 bar (73 psi). If the pressure is below 5 bar (73 psi), remove the fuel strainer from the tank, inspect the strainer for damage or blockage, and clean or replace as required.

  3. Inspect the low-pressure pump/ECU line for damage or blockage. There should be no damage or blockage.

    1. If damage or blockage is found, blow the line out or repair the fuel line as necessary.

    2. If no blockage or damage is found, continue to the next step.

  4. Check the status of the following related faults:

    SPN-62021-31 / DTC-F245-1F

    Fuel Pressure Relief Valve: Pressure Increase Forcing Relief Valve Open

    SPN-62277-31 / DTC-F345-1F

    Fuel Pressure Relief Valve: Pressure Spike Forcing Relief Valve Open

    SPN-21077-31 / DTC-5255-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Open Circuit

    SPN-16725 / DTC-4155

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Intermittent Electrical Connection Failure

    SPN-33621-31 / DTC-8355-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Over-Temperature Error

    SPN-15461-31 / DTC-3C65-1F

    High Pressure Pump Fuel Delivery Quantity at Low Speed is Too High

    SPN-15205-31 / DTC-3B65-1F

    High-Pressure Pump Fuel Delivery Quantity in Over Run Exceeds a Maximum Threshold

    SPN-8805-31 / DTC-2265-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Has Exceeded Maximum Positive Deviation Limits

    SPN-7557-31 / DTC-1D85-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure has Exceeded the Maximum Limit During Limp Home Mode

    SPN-5157-31 / DTC-1425-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Sensor: Voltage is Too High

    SPN-5605 / DTC-15E5

    Prefilter Fuel Pressure Sensor Voltage is Higher than Expected

    1. If any of the listed faults are active, resolve them first and then determine if this fault is also resolved.

    2. If the fault has not been resolved, replace the PRV, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to perform the Component Replacement > Replace Rail PRV procedure. Erase all fault codes and return the machine to service.
