SPN-4961-31 / DTC-1361-1F

ECU Internal Voltage 5V, Transfer Box: Battery Voltage is Too High

Document ID: 5836 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Sep 10, 2025

Fault Description: 

The electronic control unit (ECU) monitors battery voltage. If the ECU determines that battery voltage is more than 35.7 V, this fault occurs.

Cause: 

Battery voltage monitored at the ECU is more than 35.7 V.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Faulty charging system.

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fault is present and active using either the PT-Box tool or LogOn, then retrieve the IQAN clone.

    1. If the fault is present and active, continue to the next step.

    2. If the fault is no longer present or in an inactive state, the fault may be intermittent. Continue to Step 4.

  2. Check the battery voltage.

    With the key in the STOP position, use a multimeter to perform the following voltage check:

    From

    To

    Value

    Battery (+) Terminal

    Battery (–) Terminal

    Approximately 24 V

    1. If the voltage is approximately 24 V, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help.

    2. If the voltage is not approximately 24 V, continue to the next step.

  3. Check the charging system for proper operation.

    1. If the charging system is not functioning properly, repair the charging system as necessary.

    2. If the charging system is functioning properly, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help.

  4. Visually inspect the relevant harnesses and connectors for bent or dislocated pins, corroded terminals, broken wires, and signs of heat damage or pinch damage to the harness braiding. Verify that the connectors are fully installed. Flex the harnesses involved to reveal intermittent breaks or shorts in the wiring. Operate the machine while you monitor the display.

    1. If you find damage or the display indicates abnormal display readings, repair the damage discovered during the inspection or locate and repair the abnormal display condition, then verify that the fault has been resolved.

    2. If you do not find damage and the display indicates only normal readings, delete the fault code and return the machine to service.
