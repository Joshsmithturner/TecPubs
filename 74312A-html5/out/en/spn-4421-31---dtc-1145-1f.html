SPN-4421-31 / DTC-1145-1F

Fuel Pressure Relief Valve Reached Maximum Allowed Opening Count

Document ID: 615 | Version: 4.3 | Last Modified Date: Sep 10, 2025

Fault Description: 

A mechanical pressure relief valve (PRV), integrated in the fuel rail, opens if excessive pressure is present in the rail. The electronic control unit (ECU) detects and counts the sudden pressure drops in the fuel rail due to an opening of the PRV. If more than 50 are detected, this fault occurs.

Cause: 

The PRV opening counter internal to the ECU has exceeded 50 counts of the relief opening.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Faulty PRV.

  2. Faulty fuel metering valve (MPROP), wiring.

  3. Faulty fuel metering valve (MPROP), internal failure.

Suggested Spare Parts:

  • PRV

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fault is present and active using either the PT-Box tool or LogOn, then retrieve the IQAN clone.

  2. Check the tightness of the PRV and torque to 102 N-m (75 ft-lbs).

  3. Check the status of the following related faults:

    SPN-17509-31 / DTC-4465-1F

    Fuel Pump Pressure has Exceeded Desired Pressure Limits

    SPN-6245-31 / DTC-1865-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure has Exceeded the Maximum Limit

    SPN-21077-31 / DTC-5255-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Open Circuit

    SPN-16725 / DTC-4155

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Intermittent Electrical Connection Failure

    SPN-8805-31 / DTC-2265-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Has Exceeded Maximum Positive Deviation Limits

    SPN-5477-31 / DTC-1565-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Has Exceeded Maximum Negative Deviation Limits

    1. If any of the listed faults are active, diagnose them first and then return to this fault.

    2. If none of the listed faults are active, continue to the next step.

  4. Replace the PRV and torque to 102 N-m (75 ft-lbs), use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to perform the Component Replacement > Replace Rail PRV procedure, and then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.

    1. If the fault has been resolved, return the machine to service.

    2. If the fault is not resolved, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help.

      Please have the following information ready:

      • Machine serial number

      • Machine hours

      • IQAN clone

      • FPT fault codes and stored data from the PT-Box or LogOn
