Check the Cooling System

Document ID: 4798 | Version: 2.4 | Last Modified Date: Oct 28, 2024

Required Tools:

  • Coolant system pressure testing tool (includes pressure pump, radiator cap adapter, and quick-connect)

Preparation:

  • Ignition key is in the STOP position.

  • Sufficient coolant liquid is available at the coolant surge tank

Procedure:

  1. Do a static pressure test with the engine off.

    1. STOP the engine and make sure that the engine is not hot.

    2. Carefully remove the radiator cap from the surge tank, being aware of any trapped or hot coolant.

    3. Make sure that the coolant in the surge tank is at the recommended level and the coolant quality looks good.

    4. Select a suitable cap adapter, wet its rubber gasket with water or coolant, and fit it carefully to the surge tank. Make sure that the adapter cap properly seals the surge tank.

    5. Install the pressure pump onto the adapter using the quick-connect.

    6. Using the pump, pressurize the system to 124 kPa (18 psi)

    7. When the pressure reaches the determined pressure value, stop pumping and watch the pump pressure gauge. If the pressure drops below 97 kPa (14 psi), this indicates a leak in the system.

    8. With the system still pressurized, check the radiator and housing for external leaks.

    9. After the test is completed, depressurize the coolant system and fill the surge tank to the correct coolant level. Install the radiator cap on the surge tank.

  2. Do a dynamic pressure test with the engine on.

    1. STOP the engine and make sure that the engine is not hot.

    2. Carefully remove the radiator cap from the surge tank, being aware of any trapped or hot coolant.

    3. Make sure that the coolant in the surge tank is at the recommended level and the coolant quality looks good.

    4. Select a suitable cap adapter, wet its rubber gasket with water or coolant, and fit it carefully to the surge tank. Make sure that the adapter cap properly seals the surge tank.

    5. Install the pressure pump onto the adapter using the quick-connect.

    6. START the engine at low speed and watch the pump pressure gauge.

      If there is a rapid increase in coolant pressure, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help. A rapid increase in coolant pressure indicates a damaged cylinder head or cylinder head gasket, which leaks cylinder pressure into the coolant system.

    7. After the test is completed, STOP the engine, depressurize the coolant system, and fill the surge tank to the correct coolant level. Refit the radiator cap to the surge tank.

  3. Check the coolant surge tank.

    1. If coolant is leaking from the radiator cap while the engine is running:

      • The cooling system pressure could be too high.

      • The coolant level in the surge tank could be too high with not enough room for expansion.

      • The radiator cap could be faulty.

    2. While performing either the static or dynamic pressure test on the cooling system, check for leaks at the following surge tank locations:

      • Welds

      • Seams

      • Coolant Level Sensor

      • Sight Glass

      • Hose Clamps

  4. Visually check the coolant temperature sensor for damage and correct functionality.

    1. Check the sensor connector for the following:

      • Damage to the sensor wiring

      • Damage or corrosion on the sensor contacts

      • Correct sensor functionality according to the resistance/temperature correlation table:

        Coolant Temperature Sensor Resistance

        Coolant Temperature

        2.40 kΩ

        20°C (68°F)

        0.98 kΩ

        45°C  (113°F)

        0.66 kΩ

        60°C (140°F)

        0.44 kΩ

        70°C (158°F)

        0.32 kΩ

        80°C (176°F)

  5. Read out the coolant temperature sensor value.

    With the engine stopped and cold, turn the key to the RUN position and use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to read out the coolant temperature sensor value. The value should be equal to the environmental/ambient temperature.

  6. Visually check the cooling system.

    1. START the engine and check if the fan is working correctly.

    2. STOP the engine and visually check the cooling system for damage:

      • Check the drive belt condition.

      • Check the fan blade condition.

      • Check the water pump condition.

      • Check the cooler assembly condition.

  7. Mechanically check the water pump.

    1. START the engine and check the water pump mechanical sounds.

    2. STOP the engine, remove the drive belt, and check the water pump pulley functionality.

    3. Check the water pump for leaks or damage.

  8. Check the fan activation.

    Refer to the machine's service manual for fan-related checks.

  9. Check the engine temperature adjustment.

    1. Check that the battery voltage is sufficient.

    2. Check that the vehicle fuses are okay.

    3. Check that the connectors and plugs are okay. There should be no loose contact or corrosion.

    4. START the engine and connect the PT-Box tool.

    5. Use the PT-Box tool to simulate the change of coolant temperature. It can be varied from 0–100°C (32–212°F).

      The engine temperature adjustment will show a Des. Temp gauge (Desired Temperature) and a Water Temp gauge (Water Temperature) and will product the following results:

      • Pressing the Increase (+) button will increase the Desired Temperature gauge by increments of 10, which is then matched by the Water Temperature gauge.

      • Pressing the Decrease (−) button will decrease the Desired Temperature gauge by increments of 10, which is then matched by the Water Temperature gauge.

      • Pressing the red STOP button on the right side of the screen will cancel the simulation.

    6. If the engine temperature gauge on the machine does not match the temperature adjustment on the PT-Box, check J1939 communication between the ECU and the IQAN modules.
