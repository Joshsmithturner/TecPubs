Check Suction Pressure After Pre-Filter (Fuel/Water Separator)

Document ID: 5231 | Version: 2.3 | Last Modified Date: Sep 10, 2025

Required Tools:

  • Fuel Circuit Pressure Check Kit (212542)

  • Cursor Engine Fuel Leakage Test Kit Add-On (Part Number 73470B)

  • Low pressure vacuum gauge

Procedure:

  1. Prepare to check the suction pressure.

    A

    Outlet from Fuel Pre-Filter (Fuel/Water Separator)

    B

    Tee Hose

    C

    Low Pressure Gauge

    D

    Inlet to ECU Cooling Plate

    Note

    The hose routing and pre-filter location will vary based on the machine configuration.

    • Carry out a visual inspection of the components for any signs of leakage.

    • Install the low pressure gauge between the pre-filter outlet and the ECU cooling plate inlet.

    • Tighten all connections to make sure that there is no possibility of leakage.

  2. Check the suction pressure at the pre-filter outlet.

    1. Start the engine and let it run for several minutes.

    2. In case of non-starter: Crank the engine multiple times (maximum 10 seconds each) until the engine has run for a total of 30 seconds.

      Notice

      To prevent damaging the starter, do not engage the starter motor for more than 10–12 seconds each time. Wait 30 seconds between each attempt to START the engine.

    3. Observe the suction pressure in several engine conditions and note the values while looking for leaks. The pressures should match the values in the table below.

      Engine Condition

      Minimum Pressure (Relative)

      Maximum Pressure (Relative)

      Low Speed (900 rpm)

      –0.2 bar (–3 psi)

      0 bar (0 psi)

      High Speed (1500 rpm)

      –0.2 bar (–3 psi)

      0 bar (0 psi)

  3. Check if the pressure measured is in the range explained in the table above.

    1. If the expected results are NOT OK (suction pressure too low, above maximum values):

      • Check the tightness of tubes, and connectors. Any small defect in tightness will lead to reduced suction pressure. Air will be sucked into the system and the diesel flow will be reduced. Check that there are no air bubbles in the backflow from the pump.

      • Insufficient diesel fuel in the tank.

      • Check the pre-filter for blockage or damage and the main filter for blockage or damage. Blocked or contaminated filters can reduce the suction pressure.

      • Rare but possible: The gear pump is defective (exclude the possibility of other hydraulic components before making this conclusion).

      Locate and correct the cause of the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the expected results are NOT OK (suction pressure too high, below minimum values):

      • Check the pre-filter for blockage or damage and the main filter for blockage or damage. Blocked or contaminated filters can reduce the suction pressure.

      • Tubes and connectors are squeezed/damaged/wrong diameter.

      • The fuel tank system is dirty.

      • Tank ventilation is blocked/dirty (open tank cap, and listen to sucking sound).

      Locate and correct the cause of the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    3. If the expected results are OK, continue to the next step.

  4. The system works properly.
