Check the Intake Air System

Document ID: 4642 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Sep 12, 2024

Preparation:

  • Ignition key is in the STOP position.

  • Air filter is clean.

Procedure:

  1. Visually check the air filter and housing.

    A

    Air Filter Housing

    B

    Safety Filter Element

    C

    Primary Filter Element

    D

    Air Filter Cover

    E

    Cover/Housing Clamps

    • Check the air filter cover/housing clamps for correct fitting and tightness.

    • Remove the air filter cover.

    • Visually check the air filter and housing for any contamination.

    • Visually check the air filter and housing for any physical damage.

    1. If the air filter and housing are NOT OK, investigate these possible root causes:

      • Clamps are loose.

      • Air filter is clogged, causing restricted air flow.

      • Air filter housing is damaged.

      Locate and correct the cause of the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the air filter and housing are OK, continue to the next step.

  2. Visually check the air pipes for correct fittings, tightness, and damage.

    1. If the air pipes are not OK, investigate these possible root causes:

      • Air pipes are damaged.

      • Air pipes are not installed correctly.

      Locate and correct the cause of the damage, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to clear all fault codes and verify that the fault is resolved.

    2. If the air pipes are OK, continue to the next step.

  3. The intake air system works properly.
