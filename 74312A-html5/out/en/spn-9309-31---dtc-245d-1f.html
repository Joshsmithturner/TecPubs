SPN-9309-31 / DTC-245D-1F

ECU Temperature Sensor 2: Temperature is Too Low

Document ID: 6551 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Oct 24, 2024

Fault Description: 

The electronic control unit (ECU) monitors the internal temperature through one or more temperature sensors. If temperature sensor 2 detects a temperature lower than the set temperature, this fault occurs.

Cause: 

The internal ECU temperature is below the minimum acceptable level.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Low ambient temperature.

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fault is present and active using either the PT-Box tool or LogOn, then retrieve the IQAN clone.

    1. If the fault is present and active, continue to the next step.

    2. If the fault is no longer present or in an inactive state, the fault may be intermittent. Continue to Step 3.

  2. Check that the ambient temperature is above −60°C (−76°F).

    1. If the ambient temperature is less than −60°C (−76°F), warm the machine up, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.

    2. If the ambient temperature is greater than −60°C (−76°F), continue to the next step.

  3. With the key in the STOP position, verify that the wiring harness and connectors are free of damage.

    • Verify that the negative battery connections from the battery to the frame are tight and secure.

    • Verify that the connections from the frame ground to the ECU are tight and secure.

    • Verify that the connections from the battery to the ECU are tight and secure.

    • Verify that the harness is free of damage, abrasion, corrosion, and incorrect attachment from the battery to the ECU.

    1. If the wiring harness is not damaged and all the connections are secure, replace the ECU, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.

    2. If the wiring harness is damaged or the connectors are loose or damaged, repair or replace the wiring as required, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.
