SPN-5957-31 / DTC-1745-1F

Pressure Relief Valve Reached Maximum Allowed Opening Time

Document ID: 4423 | Version: 2.2 | Last Modified Date: Sep 10, 2025

Note

Because this fault causes inducement, it is necessary to perform the Inducement Unlock/Counter Reset once the fault has been resolved. Contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department if needed.

Fault Description: 

A mechanical pressure relief valve (PRV), integrated in the fuel rail, opens if excessive pressure is present in the rail. The electronic control unit (ECU) detects, counts, and records the sudden pressure drops in the fuel rail due to an opening of the PRV, and the duration of its opening. The duration of opening is accumulated in every driving cycle. If the PRV is open for more than a total of 5.0 hours, this fault occurs.

Cause: 

The ECU has recorded an opening of the PRV longer than the maximum permitted time of 5.0 hours.

Possible Failure Modes:

  1. Faulty fuel injection system, causing the PRV to open for an extended period of time.

  2. Faulty low-pressure fuel lines, clogged fuel filter, faulty charge gear pump, or faulty lift pump (if equipped).

  3. Faulty fuel metering valve (MPROP), internal failure or wiring.

  4. Faulty PRV, mechanical failure.

  5. Faulty fuel rail pressure sensor, implausible value.

Suggested Spare Parts:

  • Fuel metering valve (MPROP)

  • PRV

  • Fuel rail pressure sensor

Solution:

  1. Verify that the fault is present and active using either the PT-Box tool or LogOn, then retrieve the IQAN clone.

  2. Check the tightness of the PRV and torque to 102 N-m (75 ft-lbs).

  3. Check the status of the following related faults:

    SPN-4645-31 / DTC-1225-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Sensor: Raw Value is Above Maximum Offset

    SPN-5157-31 / DTC-1425-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Sensor: Voltage is Too High

    SPN-6245-31 / DTC-1865-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure has Exceeded the Maximum Limit

    SPN-9509-31 / DTC-2525-1F

    Fuel Rail Pressure Sensor: Voltage is Too Low

    SPN-21077-31 / DTC-5255-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Open Circuit

    SPN-26197-31 / DTC-6655-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Short Circuit to Battery on Low Side

    SPN-30037-31 / DTC-7555-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Short Circuit to Ground of the Supply Voltage

    SPN-30549-31 / DTC-7755-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Short Circuit to Ground on Low Side

    SPN-33621-31 / DTC-8355-1F

    Fuel Metering Valve (MPROP): Over-Temperature Error

    SPN-59101-31 / DTC-E6DD-1F

    ECU Internal: Diagnosis Fault Check to Report the Plausibility Error in Fuel Rail Pressure Monitoring

    1. If any of the listed faults are active, diagnose them first and then return to this fault.

    2. If none of the listed faults are active, continue to the next step.

  4. Visually check and inspect the low pressure side of the fuel supply system to check that there are no leaks, obstructions, or damage due to air entry. Inspect the gear pump to verify that the pressure is correct.

    Check operation of the fuel rail pressure sensor.

    1. If leakage, clogging, air entry, damage, or low gear pump pressure is found, replace fittings, filters, lines, or other damaged parts as needed.

    2. If no leakage, clogging, air entry, damage, or low gear pump pressure is found, continue to the next step.

  5. Replace the fuel metering valve (MPROP), use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to perform the Component Replacement > Replace Rail Metering Unit procedure, and then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.

    1. If the fault has been resolved, use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to perform the Inducement Unlock/Counter Reset, then return the machine to service.

    2. If the fault is not resolved, continue to the next step.

  6. Replace the PRV and torque to 102 N-m (75 ft-lbs), use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to perform the Component Replacement > Replace Rail PRV procedure, and then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.

    1. If the fault has been resolved, use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to perform the Inducement Unlock/Counter Reset, then return the machine to service.

    2. If the fault is not resolved, continue to the next step.

  7. Replace the fuel rail pressure sensor, then use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to verify that the fault has been resolved.

    1. If the fault has been resolved, use the PT-Box tool or LogOn to perform the Inducement Unlock/Counter Reset, then return the machine to service.

    2. If the fault is not resolved, contact the Tigercat Industries Service Department for more help.
